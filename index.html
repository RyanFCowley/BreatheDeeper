Breathe Deeper

How clean is your air?

This web-app shows the air quality across the USA and how it relates to the population density of each state. The coloured dots represent each air monitoring station in the US. Hovering within a state boundry displays that states name and the population density of people per square mile. The term 'AQI' stands for 'Air Quality Index' and is measured in PM2.5 - basically, particulate matter density or pollution in the air. The colour of the dots relates to the level of pollution:

0-25 AQI, Almost or completely clean air

25-50 AQI, Satisfactory air quality

50-100 AQI, May effect extremely sensitive groups

100-150 AQI, Members of sensitive groups affected

150-200 AQI, General public risk health concerns

200-250 AQI, Entire pop. face serious health risks

250-300 AQI, Just dont go outside
